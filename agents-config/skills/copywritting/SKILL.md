Use this skill when the user asks for copywriting, copy, accroches, headlines, email marketing text, landing-page text, VSL/script, sales-page structure, offer wording, CTA, persuasion, or conversion-focused rewriting.

Default posture:
- Mirror the user's language. For Melvynx-style French, write in direct `tu`, short punchy paragraphs, practical proof, and a clear CTA.
- Preserve the user's voice unless they explicitly ask for a new tone. Improve clarity, rhythm, specificity, and persuasion without making the text sound generic.
- Diagnose before rewriting: who is the reader, what pain/desire is active, what belief must change, what action is wanted, and what objection blocks it.
- Prefer one strong promise over several medium claims. Remove weak arguments that dilute credibility.
- Never fake proof, urgency, scarcity, testimonials, authority, or guarantees. If a proof point is missing, mark it as needed.

Core operating loop:
1. Identify the single objective: open, click, subscribe, book, buy, reply, or trust.
2. Define the prospect in concrete terms: current situation, desired identity, pain avoided, pleasure gained, objections, language they already use.
3. Build the slippery slide: every line must make the next line easier to read.
4. Sell the concept before the product: make the reader want the transformation, then make the product the credible bridge.
5. Translate features into benefits, then into lived experience: `feature -> mechanism -> benefit -> identity/status`.
6. Add proof and risk reversal: specificity, examples, mechanism, testimonials, guarantee, credible limits.
7. Cut 50-80% when possible. Keep rhythm: short line, medium line, short line, then a precise detail.
8. End with a direct CTA and remove competing actions.

Quality bar before answering:
- The first line creates attention or relevance.
- The promise is specific enough to be believed.
- The reader understands what's in it for them before seeing product details.
- Any urgency/scarcity has a reason.
- The copy answers the obvious objections before the CTA.
- The final output is usable as-is, not just advice.
