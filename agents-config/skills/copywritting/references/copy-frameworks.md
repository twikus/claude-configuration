# Copywriting Frameworks

## WIIFM

Every section must answer: `What's in it for me?`

Practical rules:
- Talk about the reader's problem before your solution.
- Use more `tu/vous/you` than `je/nous/we`, unless the task is personal storytelling.
- Make the reader visualize the lived experience after the click, signup, or purchase.
- Show pain avoided and pleasure gained.

## Benefits Vs Features

Feature: what the product contains.
Benefit: what the feature makes possible.
Mechanism: why the benefit is credible.
Identity: what it says about the buyer.

Use this chain:
`feature -> mechanism -> benefit -> proof -> identity`

Example:
`3 guided projects -> you apply each concept immediately -> you stop forgetting tutorials -> shipped apps prove progress -> you become the developer who builds`.

## Three Levels Of Persuasion

1. Rational: price, specs, content, duration, deliverables, guarantee.
2. Emotional: desire, frustration, relief, pride, fear avoided, confidence.
3. Identity: `people like me do this`, belonging, status, taste, ambition.

Most decisions happen emotionally or through identity, then get rationalized. Give the reader rational arguments to justify the action they already want to take.

## AIDA

Attention:
- interrupt the default scroll,
- name the exact pain,
- use a surprising belief,
- or show a concrete desirable outcome.

Interest:
- show you understand the reader's situation,
- add a mechanism,
- build curiosity with open loops.

Desire:
- make the reader picture the transformation,
- add proof and specificity,
- remove doubt.

Action:
- one clear next step,
- direct CTA,
- reason to act now,
- risk reversal if needed.

## PAS

Problem:
- state the reader's problem in their language.

Agitate:
- show the concrete consequence of leaving it unsolved.
- make the future visual, not abstract.

Solve:
- introduce the product, mechanism, or offer as the credible bridge.

Use PAS when the reader already feels pain. If they are unaware, educate before agitating.

## USP / Proposition

A strong proposition is:
- desirable,
- different,
- specific,
- concise,
- credible.

Questions to answer:
- What exact problem is solved?
- Is the problem real and painful?
- What does the customer currently do instead?
- Do they already spend money to solve it?
- If there is a free alternative, are you meaningfully better?
- What differentiator matters to this audience?
- Can the value be said in one sentence?

## Headlines

Use the 4U checklist:
- Useful: clear benefit.
- Urgent: why now.
- Unique: fresh angle.
- Ultra-specific: concrete detail, number, audience, or situation.

Headline patterns:
- `Stop [pain] without [common sacrifice]`
- `[Audience]: get [desired result] without [friction]`
- `The [mechanism] that helps [audience] [result]`
- `Why [common belief] is costing you [specific loss]`
- `I tried [specific action] so you can [benefit]`

Avoid generic hype. Specificity beats adjectives.

## Attention Techniques

Pattern interrupt:
- a surprising first sentence,
- an unexpected visual,
- a contradiction,
- a taboo but true observation,
- a concrete number.

Mirror effect:
- describe the prospect's situation so precisely they feel recognized.

Open loop:
- introduce a question or tension and delay the answer only as long as useful.

## Credibility

If one promise feels fake, the whole page weakens.

Credibility sources:
- exact proof,
- testimonials,
- case studies,
- comments,
- named clients,
- personal track record,
- teaching history,
- transparent process,
- technical detail,
- product screenshots,
- guarantee or trial,
- honest limitations.

Mechanize the promise: name the method and explain how it produces the result.

## Choice

One page should usually have one objective. Remove navigation, social links, and secondary actions when conversion matters.

Choice rules:
- 1 offer is clearest.
- 3 options can work if they are genuinely distinct.
- Too many similar options lower conversion and satisfaction.

## Rewrite Checklist

- Keep the user's style.
- Shorten sentences.
- Replace vague words with concrete words.
- Add `because` where claims need a reason.
- Turn features into outcomes.
- Answer one objection.
- Make the CTA visible and direct.
