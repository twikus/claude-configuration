# Email, Landing Page, Ads, VSL

## Newsletter Editing

For Melvynx-style newsletters:
- Correct grammar and spelling.
- Preserve voice, opinions, rhythm, and directness.
- Make phrases shorter and more impactful.
- Do not over-polish into corporate French.
- Keep the same Markdown/HTML/email tags.
- Strengthen hook, transitions, and CTA.

Useful structure:
1. Hook: exact pain, belief, story, or curiosity.
2. Context: why it matters now.
3. Lesson: one useful idea.
4. Proof/example: personal, technical, or customer-specific.
5. CTA: one simple action.

## Marketing Email

Rules:
- Subject must create curiosity or clear benefit.
- Sender name must be recognizable.
- First line must justify the open.
- Use personal tone; email is a relationship channel.
- Give value before selling.
- Keep it brief when the job is to click.
- Use the PS; it is often read.
- One email, one main CTA.

Email sequence ideas:
- story/origin,
- problem diagnosis,
- mechanism,
- proof,
- objection,
- offer,
- urgency,
- last chance.

Launch / relance arc (condensed):
- Day 1: announce the transformation and exactly who it is for.
- Middle: proof, objections, mechanism, examples, personal reason to act.
- Final: urgency, recap, guarantee, and what happens right after buying.

## Landing Page

A landing page is not a generic homepage. It has one clear action.

Before writing:
- Channel: where does traffic come from?
- Target: who suffers most from the hard problem and can pay?
- Value proposition: what action are they trying to complete?
- Message: how would the audience describe the problem in their own words?

Research rule: call customers, record calls, and reuse their real jargon.

Landing structure:
1. Hero: audience + promise + mechanism + CTA.
2. Problem: mirror the current painful situation.
3. Stakes: what happens if nothing changes.
4. Solution: concept first, product second.
5. Mechanism: why this works.
6. Benefits: concrete outcomes, not feature list.
7. Proof: testimonials, screenshots, numbers, examples.
8. Offer: what is included, who it is for, price if relevant.
9. Objections: FAQ or direct rebuttals.
10. CTA: repeated after major belief shifts.

Long copy is fine when price, risk, or complexity is high.

## Ads

Cold traffic:
- first 1-2 seconds must stop attention,
- 10-40 seconds to interest, create desire, answer a key objection,
- 1-3 seconds for CTA.

Warm traffic:
- shorter,
- more direct,
- proof or offer-heavy.

Creative testing:
- change one big variable at a time, usually the hook first.
- Analyze data, avatar, and competitors.
- Use Facebook Ads Library or competitor swipe files for inspiration.

AIDA timing for short video:
- Attention: first 5 seconds.
- Interest: around 12 seconds.
- Desire: around 20 seconds.
- Action: before 30-60 seconds.

## VSL

VSL means video sales letter. Use it when voice, story, and belief change matter more than a static page.

Core arc:
1. Pattern interrupt / hook.
2. Name the painful current state.
3. Make the reader/viewer feel understood.
4. Introduce a new belief.
5. Explain the mechanism.
6. Prove the mechanism.
7. Present the offer.
8. Remove objections.
9. Give a direct CTA.

## CTA

A CTA should be direct, concrete, and tied to the desired next step.

Weak: `Learn more`
Better: `Rejoins la formation`
Better with context: `Commencer le module 1 aujourd'hui`

CTA checks:
- Is the action obvious?
- Is the value of clicking obvious?
- Is there only one primary action?
- Is the risk clear or reversed?
