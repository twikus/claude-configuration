# Brand, Positioning, MVB

## Minimum Viable Branding

Define enough brand to guide decisions without building a bloated brand book.

Core fields:
- Values: how the business behaves.
- Product value: why customers choose it.
- Enemies: the idea, status quo, or situation the brand fights.
- Weapons: strengths, knowledge, product advantages, culture, process.
- Mission: why the business exists.
- Appearance: visual/cultural world the brand belongs to.
- Customers: no more than three primary target groups; stack-rank them.
- Differentiators: unique x-factors competitors cannot easily copy.
- Brand ethos: the concise North Star connecting goals, mission, vision, and culture.

## Why / How / What

Why:
- What world do we believe in?
- What are we fighting for?
- What are we fighting against?
- What real, obvious, hard problem do we solve?

How:
- What is our mission?
- What weapons do we use?
- What mantra or rallying cry captures it?

What:
- What do we build or provide?
- What is the killer feature?
- What unfair advantage supports it?

## Positioning Axes

Map competitors on two meaningful axes. Look for underserved corners.

Examples of axes:
- simple vs powerful,
- beginner vs expert,
- fast vs deep,
- premium vs accessible,
- community-led vs self-serve,
- technical depth vs business outcome.

Choose the corner where the product can be the obvious best choice for a specific audience.

## Differentiation

Good differentiation is:
- relevant to the audience,
- hard to copy,
- easy to understand,
- connected to a painful problem,
- visible in product and messaging.

Possible differentiators:
- language/community,
- teaching style,
- speed,
- practical projects,
- founder story,
- platform,
- updates,
- support,
- opinionated method,
- niche specificity.

## Story And Culture

A strong brand gives customers a story to tell about themselves.

Ask:
- What do `people like us` do?
- What behavior do we normalize?
- What old world do we reject?
- What status does the customer gain?
- What community or movement do they join?

## Landing Message Fit

Landing-page positioning should come from:
- channel,
- target,
- value proposition,
- problem language,
- customer jargon from calls,
- one action.

Do not write a generic homepage when the goal is conversion.
