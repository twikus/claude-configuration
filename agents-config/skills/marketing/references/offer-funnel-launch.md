# Offer, Funnel, Launch

## Offer Design

A strong offer combines:
- desirable outcome,
- credible mechanism,
- clear scope,
- fast first win,
- proof,
- risk reversal,
- price logic,
- real urgency or scarcity,
- simple buying path.

Start by deciding the price, then create enough value to make the price feel obvious.

## The Value Equation

```
            Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value =  ----------------------------------------------------------
                Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice
```

To make an offer feel irresistible, push the numerator up and the denominator down:

- **Dream Outcome (up):** sell the biggest credible transformation and the identity behind it.
- **Perceived likelihood (up):** proof, mechanism, testimonials, guarantee, track record. The reader must believe it will work *for them*.
- **Time delay (down):** show the fast first win; shorten time-to-result or make early progress visible.
- **Effort & sacrifice (down):** done-for-you parts, templates, clear steps, support, remove friction and overwhelm.

When an offer feels weak, diagnose which of the four terms is failing and fix that one before adding bonuses.

Use this offer stack:
1. Core transformation.
2. Main deliverables.
3. Support or feedback.
4. Bonuses that remove objections.
5. Guarantee or risk reversal.
6. Deadline, limited cohort, or reason to act now if true.

## Atomic Funnel

Practical sequence:
1. Find the idea.
2. Brainstorm before/after marketing.
3. Sell through a small product first; V2 is easier than V1.
4. Define price early.
5. Build content around the transformation.
6. Use ads and swipe research for angles.
7. Run AIDA in the creative.

Always add `pour` / `for` in training topics to connect content to the user's outcome.

## Launch Formula

Pre-launch sequence:

Video 1: Problem and opportunity
- name the common problem,
- show frustrations and stakes,
- prove you understand,
- tease a new opportunity,
- invite them to follow the next piece.

Video 2: Transformation and solution
- show before/after,
- show examples or testimonials,
- reveal some of the method without giving everything,
- build desire and expectation.

Video 3: Offer and action
- present the offer as the bridge,
- explain modules/steps,
- show bonuses/support,
- add real urgency,
- direct CTA.

## Challenge Funnel

Use challenges when the market needs proof through experience.

Good challenge:
- short duration,
- one concrete promise,
- daily action,
- public momentum,
- visible progress,
- next-step offer at the end.

Example challenges: building a small app in a week, or going from idea to shipped SaaS in a defined window.

## Ads Funnel

Budget logic by traffic temperature:
- Cold traffic: majority of budget; introduce problem and desire.
- Warm traffic: people who engaged but did not visit/convert.
- Hot traffic: site visitors and leads.
- Customers: sell next product or retention offer.

Creative shape:
- 1-2 seconds: attention.
- 10-40 seconds: interest, desire, objections.
- 1-3 seconds: CTA.

When iterating, change the hook first before rewriting everything.

## Urgency And Scarcity

Only use urgency when there is a real reason:
- cohort closes,
- live support starts,
- price changes,
- bonus expires,
- seats are limited by support capacity,
- launch window ends.

If urgency is high, all major questions must already be answered.
