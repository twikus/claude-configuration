# One-Page Marketing / Direct Response

## Strategy Before Tactics

Do not start by choosing ads, posts, or tools. First decide:
- target market,
- message,
- media/channel,
- offer,
- follow-up,
- conversion path,
- economics.

Marketing is the planned system that gets the ideal target market to know, like, trust, and buy.

## The 9-Square Plan (3 Phases)

The 1-Page Marketing Plan maps the prospect lifecycle across three phases, three squares each. Fill all nine before spending on tactics.

**Before (prospect, not yet aware) - goal: get them to know you and respond.**
1. Target market: the specific niche you serve (use PVP to choose).
2. Message: the offer + USP crafted for that market's pain and language.
3. Media: the channel(s) used to reach them.

**During (lead, knows you) - goal: get them to like, trust, and buy.**
4. Lead capture: capture details so you can follow up (free report, quiz, challenge).
5. Lead nurture: educate and build trust over multiple touches.
6. Sales conversion: the offer, proof, and risk reversal that turn a warm lead into a buyer.

**After (customer, has bought) - goal: maximize lifetime value and referrals.**
7. World-class experience: deliver a result worth talking about.
8. Increase lifetime value: upsells, ascension, recurring, reactivation.
9. Orchestrate referrals: make it natural and rewarding to refer others.

Most businesses obsess over square 6 (the sale) and neglect 4-5 (capture/nurture) and 7-9 (LTV/referrals), where most of the profit actually lives.

## Product Quality Is Retention, Not Acquisition

A good product helps retention, referrals, and word of mouth. But a prospect only discovers quality after buying. Acquisition still needs marketing.

Do not rely on `if we build it, they will come`.

## Target Market

Broad targeting dilutes the message. The ad should make the prospect think: `this is for me`.

Use PVP scoring:
- Personal fulfillment: do you enjoy serving this segment?
- Value to marketplace: does this segment value the work?
- Profitability: does this segment produce real profit?

Choose a segment where all three are strong.

## Direct Response Principles

Direct response marketing is `money at a discount`: spend less to acquire profitably measurable sales or leads.

Direct response campaigns:
- are trackable,
- are measurable,
- target a specific audience,
- make a specific offer,
- demand a response,
- capture contact details when possible,
- include follow-up,
- use time/quantity limits only when real.

## One Ad, One Objective

Every ad, email, or page should have one objective. Remove anything that does not help that objective, even sacred elements like logo placement or extra navigation when they distract.

Examples of objectives:
- request free report,
- join waitlist,
- book call,
- start trial,
- buy,
- reply,
- watch video.

## Capture Before Conversion

When direct sale creates too much resistance, invite the prospect to raise their hand:
- free report,
- checklist,
- challenge,
- webinar,
- diagnostic,
- quiz,
- template,
- mini-course.

Then follow up with education, proof, objections, and a second offer.

## Follow-Up

Most conversion happens after the first touch. Build multi-step follow-up:
- email,
- retargeting,
- calls,
- community,
- content,
- reminders,
- deadline sequence.

Each follow-up should carry one idea and one next step.

## Metrics

Track:
- traffic source,
- opt-in rate,
- cost per lead,
- email open/click/reply,
- sales conversion,
- refund/churn,
- lifetime value,
- payback period.

Drop, edit, or retest tactics that do not return value.
