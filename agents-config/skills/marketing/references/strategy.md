# Marketing Strategy

## Definition

Marketing is the generous act of helping a specific group solve a real problem and move toward a desired change. It is not just ads, branding, or pressure. It is the strategy that makes the right people know, like, trust, and choose the offer.

Useful distinction:
- Tactics: ads, posts, landing pages, emails, webinars, launches.
- Strategy: the audience, change, story, promise, culture, positioning, and trust built over time.

## Ethical Marketing

Use marketing to solve the audience's problem, not to use the audience to solve the business's problem.

Rules:
- Seek volunteers, not victims.
- Do not spam people who did not ask to hear from you.
- Build trust with people who accept the culture.
- Help before asking.
- Create something people would miss if it disappeared.
- Be proud of the marketing.

## The Five-Step Marketing Model

1. Invent something worth making, with a story worth telling.
2. Build it so a small group gets a real benefit.
3. Tell a story that fits the inner monologue and dreams of the smallest viable market.
4. Become genuinely enthusiastic and help the story spread.
5. Show up consistently, generously, and for years to earn trust and leadership.

## Smallest Viable Market

Do not market to everyone. Find the smallest group that:
- shares a worldview,
- wants the same change,
- uses similar language,
- has a painful enough problem,
- can recognize each other as `people like us`.

The goal is not to be best for everyone. It is to be the best choice for a specific corner of the map.

Useful prompt:
`For people who desire [desire] and believe [belief], [product] is the right choice because [mechanism/difference].`

## Change And Tension

Marketing creates change. To create change, identify the current status quo and the tension that can move someone.

Tension sources:
- `You are missing out.`
- `People like us do this.`
- `Your current method is costing you.`
- `There is a better identity available.`
- `The future is already shifting.`

Avoid trying to force people who do not want the change. Find the people already leaning toward it.

## Status, Belonging, And Identity

People buy more than tasks and gadgets. They buy:
- status,
- power,
- connections,
- belonging,
- confidence,
- a story about who they are.

Ask:
- What does buying this let the customer say about themselves?
- What group do they join?
- What old identity do they leave?
- What status risk do they avoid?

## Empathy With Opinions

Different people want different things. Some love novelty; others hate change. Some want speed; others want safety. Accept that not everyone should be convinced.

Use criticism to improve targeting. A product can be wrong for some people and perfect for the niche.

## Distribution Before Selling

If people already listen, selling becomes easier. Build distribution through:
- content,
- community,
- email list,
- useful free assets,
- customer success,
- referrals,
- public building,
- recurring presence.

Marketing should make the audience more ready before the sale.
